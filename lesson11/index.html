Teaching Local Students

by April Kepner
This week our staff of meteorologists went down to a few of the local elementary schools to teach our children all about the study and science of Weather. They began with PowerPoint about how our weather technology works and with the help of some fun Graphics. They then brought a few models to give the kids a more hand-on experience, like a mini-tornado generator and a VanDergraph Machine to demonstrate the power of lighting. The reaction from students was amazing and this is something we at Action Weather want to make a yearly event. Here are a few reviews from students and the teachers.

Barney Jones:That lighting maker was fun it made my hair stand up and it was fun to shock my friends. Who knew the weather could be fun?

Sally Rose:I love the presentation from the weather people, it was awesome to see how clouds are made and why it snows so much here. It might be fun to be a meteorologists when I grow up.

Mrs. Rudd:The Presenters from Action Weather were informative and made it really easy for the kids to understand. We all try to do activities like this every year, but they do a much better job than us. I hope we can have them back next year it is great for the kids.

Fun Facts: The Soda Springs Geyser

  • The First name of this geyser was “The Oasis of Soda Springs.”
  • The geysers water is naturally carbonated because of large presents of carbon dioxide, which also makes the geyser erupts.
  • The geyser is part of a larger system of mineral springs.
  • The Soda Spring Geyser was an important landmark in the days when this part of the west was being settled. If you were a traveler on the Oregon Trail, this geyser marked the path between Fort Laramie and Fort Boise.
  • Also because of the importance of this geyser to the early settlers, it made Soda Spring the second oldest settlement in Idaho.
  • In 1937 a construction project caused this geyser, which was caped, to erupt again. Today it is both a park and visitor center, where you can come and watch the geyser go off every hour.
